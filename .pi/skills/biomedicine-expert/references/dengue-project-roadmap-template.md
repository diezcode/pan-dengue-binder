# Dengue Project Roadmap Template

Use this template when drafting project plans.

## North-star goal

Develop a safe, broadly neutralizing dengue intervention concept, ideally a pan-serotype biologic binder, that avoids or minimizes ADE and can progress through computational, non-infectious experimental, and regulated translational validation.

## Evidence hierarchy

1. Literature-supported biological target.
2. Conservation across DENV-1/2/3/4 and genotypes.
3. Structural exposure/accessibility on relevant virion states.
4. Computational binder generation and ranking.
5. Non-infectious binding/neutralization proxy assays.
6. Qualified infectious-virus neutralization by certified partners.
7. Preclinical and regulatory development.

## Decision gates

- Gate 1: validated conserved epitope.
- Gate 2: designs predicted stable and epitope-specific.
- Gate 3: recombinant binding confirmed.
- Gate 4: pseudovirus neutralization confirmed without enhancement signal.
- Gate 5: developability and safety package supports partner/regulatory engagement.

## Common red flags

- Claiming 100% conservation from four reference sequences only.
- Ignoring genotype diversity and clinical isolates.
- Designing to residues buried or inaccessible on mature virions.
- Confusing polyprotein, E-protein, and PDB residue numbering.
- Treating AlphaFold docking as proof of binding.
- Ignoring ADE, Fc receptor biology, immunogenicity, half-life, and delivery.
